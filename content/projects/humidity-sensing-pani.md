This was a group research-in-progress project on humidity sensing with Zn-doped PANI-based nanocomposites. The work combined material preparation, characterization, pellet-based measurements, and early analysis of how resistance changes under controlled relative humidity.

The page is a research note rather than a final paper: some composition labels and interpretations in the draft still need cleanup, but the experimental direction and measurement flow are clear.

## Research Goal

The project studied whether PANI composites with different Zn loading levels can act as humidity-sensitive materials. The working idea was that moisture exposure changes the material's electrical response, allowing resistance, impedance, or capacitance to be used as a humidity indicator.

The group compared pure Ag-PANI with doped samples across several concentrations, including 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, and 25%.

## Experimental Setup

Pellet samples were mounted in a holder and placed inside sealed jars that maintain known relative humidity values using saturated salt solutions.

| Salt / compound | Relative humidity |
|-----------------|------------------:|
| LiCl | 11.3% |
| K(CH3COO) | 22.7% |
| MgCl2 | 33.1% |
| K2CO3 | 43.2% |
| Mg(NO3)2 | 53.0% |
| CoCl2.6H2O | 64.0% |
| NaCl | 75.7% |
| KCl | 85.0% |
| K2SO4 | 97.6% |

Each pellet was left in a jar for a fixed interval of about 3 minutes 40 seconds before resistance was recorded with a multimeter.

## Measurements

The lab readings tracked resistance across the humidity range for pure Ag-PANI and several doped compositions. The raw values are still exploratory, but they show why repeated measurements matter: some samples change smoothly over portions of the humidity sweep, while others show non-monotonic behavior that needs retesting before claiming a sensor response.

The paper draft also includes AC response work using a Hioki 3532-50 LCR meter over 100 Hz to 1 MHz, with pellets approximately 0.8 cm in diameter and 1 mm thick.

## Characterization

The draft ties the humidity measurements to material characterization:

- PXRD was used to check structural signatures and peak changes after composite formation.
- SEM images compared surface morphology, roughness, aggregation, and porosity.
- FT-IR spectra tracked PANI ring vibrations and oxide-related bands.
- EIS, dielectric permittivity, electric modulus, and AC conductivity plots were used to study charge transport and relaxation behavior.

Those characterization results are important because humidity response depends on surface area, water adsorption sites, charge transport paths, and polymer-particle interactions.

## Status

This is still an active research note. The useful next step is to repeat the humidity sweep, clean up the material naming in the draft, and compute sensitivity and linearity for each composition so the strongest candidate can be identified quantitatively.
